Katie on Will
\nWhat were you hoping for?
\nThe closest I could get to being on First Dates \u2013 without making a fool of myself on television.<\/p>\n

First impressions?
\nAnyone who arrives earlier than me immediately gets a gold star.<\/p>\n

What did you talk about?
\nHow we both have friend groups that organise surprise trips for each other. How Irish men are funnier and more handsome (his words). How anyone without a vice is not to be trusted.My deep fear of turning 30<\/p>\n

Most awkward moment?
\nWhen Will tried to take our selfie with the camera facing the wrong way. And the knowing glances from the staff throughout the evening.<\/p>\n

Good table manners?
\nBetter than me! I wasn\u2019t going to try eating pan con tomate with a knife and fork, but Will did it gracefully.<\/p>\n

Best thing about Will?
\nAll those hobbies! Surfing, skiing, hiking. I don\u2019t think I could keep up!<\/p>\n

Would you introduce Will to your friends?
\nFor sure, our respective surprise-trip friend groups would have loads to discuss.<\/p>\n

Describe Will in three words.
\nKind, adventurous and intelligent.<\/p>\n

What do you think Will made of you?
\nWay too excited at seeing margaritas on the menu.<\/p>\n

Did you go on somewhere?
\nNo, I think the evening was winding down and we overstayed our welcome at the restaurant.<\/p>\n

And … did you kiss?
\nNo kiss: we said goodbye on the train and the fluorescent lighting wasn\u2019t quite the vibe.<\/p>\n

If you could change one thing about the evening what would it be?
\nIt was lovely weather so a second margarita on the roof terrace before dinner wouldn\u2019t have gone amiss \u2026<\/p>\n

Marks out of 10?
\n8. There wasn\u2019t that spark but I had a really fun time.<\/p>\n

Would you meet again?
\nOf course \u2013 probably as friends, it would be fun to reminisce on this experience.<\/p>\n

Best thing about Katie?
